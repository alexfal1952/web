About Me

Hi! Welcome to my webpage. I am Roberta De Vito, postdoctoral fellow at Princeton University in the Department of Computer Science, advised by Barbara Engelhardt. Previously, I was a former visiting PhD Students at Dana-Farber Cancer Institute and Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health under the supervision of Giovanni Parmigiani and Lorenzo Trippa. I graduated at University of Padua in Statistical Science in 2016 advised by Ruggero Bellio.

Research

The focus of my research is to develop statistical modeling for the analysis of big and high-throughput data, such as epidemiological and genomic data analysis. In this big picture, I use Bayesian approach and machine learning techniques to investigate biological issues, such as cancer, and depression. 

My research interests are:

  • Machine Learning

  • Email Support

  • Responsive Design

  • Consulting

  • Digital Art

  • Maintenance

  • Launch Timelines

  • Custom Branding

Currently I am working in the following applications:

•Methylation associated with depression and early puberty
•Correlation between Mother depression and teen depression
